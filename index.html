Aquaponics Viability

The United States Department of Agriculture defines aquaponics as: a combination of fish and plant production using aquaculture and hydroponics systems. A mathematical model has been developed to determine the economic viability of aquaponics systems. Considerations for this model were startup costs, operational cost, labor costs, and revenue. All of these costs were researched and models for each were developed to formulate a final model which predicts the overall profit of an aquaponics system in Pennsylvania. Changing variables within the model show which variables affect the models profitability both positively and negatively and which cause the most drastic changes. The overall model is verified using a previously developed model by Dr. Kanae Tokunaga from Oahu, Hawaii.
